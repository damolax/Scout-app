7. Controlled live acceptance tests

  1. Open Scout → Settings → Setup Readiness. Confirm contract 10.40.0 and Ready.
  2. Reconnect one Gmail sender and approve all requested permissions.
  3. Save a short test signature and click Save + sync signature. Confirm sender details say Gmail signature synced.
  4. Send one email to an address you control. Confirm the signature appears exactly once.
  5. Reply from that address. Scout → Replies → Check replies now. Confirm it appears as a real reply.
  6. Run Check replies again. Confirm no duplicate appears.
  7. Test an automatic/out-of-office reply. Confirm it appears under Automatic messages, not Real replies.
  8. Use a deliberately invalid domain in a controlled test and confirm Scout blocks it before sending or records a delivery failure without treating it as a real reply.
  9. Confirm the real reply no longer appears in the follow-up queue.
  10. Supabase → Integrations/Cron → inspect run history and confirm inbound sync calls succeed.
Do not start a large campaign until every controlled test passes. A successful local build cannot by itself prove live Google OAuth, Gmail delivery, or the exact production Supabase configuration.

8. Team handoff

  • Create the intended owner account first on a fresh installation.
  • Give team members the production URL only after Setup Readiness passes.
  • Each member connects only the Gmail accounts they are authorized to use.
  • Keep Vercel, Supabase, Google Client Secret, service key, and Cron secret private.
  • Use the in-app Team Setup page for the daily workflow and acceptance checklist.
  • After this full version is confirmed, derive the reduced no-replies/no-signature version from this exact source—not from an older codebase.
